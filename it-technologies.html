What is Cloud and what does it do?

Cloud is simply a model of computing, it is “the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the internet” (Amazon AWS, no date). Resources include access to servers and networks, storage, development tools and applications (apps) that originally would need to be stored and accessed from privately owned and managed on hard drives and servers (Accenture, no date). Cloud Servers, known as Virtual Servers, are stable and fast and secure. They have all the software they require to run and can be accessed remotely via a cloud computing platform (Probrand, no date). There are two types of cloud computing, deployment models and service categories. Deployment models consist of Public Cloud, Private Cloud, Hybrid Cloud and Multi-cloud. Service categories include, Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) (Accenture, no date). Written by Shay Hammond, Nathen Hogan and Jo Stammers

What is Cybersecurity and what does it do?

Cyber security is one of the world's most used functions of worldwide networks. Also, one of the most flawed, and easily manipulated markets. Its main purpose is to hide sensitive data, such as passwords, credit card data, phone numbers, location settings and more. Because of such an important job that cyber security plays, it is more prone to people accessing the data they didn't get explicit permission to access. The people who prey on its systems are the people who understand its integral core functions. These people are known as hackers.

what can be done now?

Some of the most common and versatile ways people interface with the cyber security world are via malicious connections against personal data. This sort of connection without known consent is called an “attack”. There are different types of attacks, some much more obvious than others.

How often have you opened your emails, and seen an email from a site that you trust offering you a gift card or free present, and all they need is your details? Quite commonly, most just click away, press block or spam, or just delete the email. But very occasionally, someone will agree and click through, and not even consider that it's a scam because “it came from a trusted site…right?” Wrong. This kind of attack is called a “phishing” attack, as it lures you in, convinces you to bite and hooks onto your data or information and reels it in. Apologies for the terrible puns, but this is quite literally what the attack does.

A similar attack usually starts the same way - opening a harmless email from a "trusted site", clicking the link to download the “coupon code” and then suddenly - a virus creeps inside and corrupts your system. This type of attack is called a malware attack. It infects your computer ware - hardware or software - with a dangerous and destructive virus. Some viruses don't actively do anything - simply read your data and send it off to its point of origin. Others actively destroy your data.

Sometimes, clicking that innocent email is all that's needed, as the hacker is already connected to the stream of communication, and acts as a “man-in-the-middle”, collecting information already passing through from you and your computer to the server of the website.

Maybe you don't even need to think about opening your emails at all, as you can't access them. Why? Because a hacker has already gotten to your password. "But how" - I hear you cry - "as I have a super secure password that I haven't told anyone!" The thing to know about hackers is that plenty of them have time and money, and programs that can scan for passwords, and if your password is simple enough to find, they will find it. The question then is; “how can I protect myself, and my users if I'm developing a site that lets them connect with people?” A very specific, measurable, achievable, realistic, and time-limited question. A SMART question if you will. If you won't… then okay I'll move on.

Some of the most common and reliable ways to protect data - especially in user-to-user via server communication - is through the help of firewalls. Firewalls are essentially traffic blocks that only let the secure information go through, by analysing which points are the safest on both ends and allowing or denying specific points of reference. Should the data be deemed safe from one user, the information passes through and gets delivered from the server. From there, it is checked again and delivered to the other user.

Machine Learning

What is Machine Learning and what does it do?

Machine learning is at its core the use of AI or “artificial intelligence” to create programs that can develop and expand upon their own pattern recognition based on user input as well as stored data from either the user or the data stored by the software itself to better understand what the users want and need, without the developer needing to specifically program the specific task. It does this by imitating the way humans learn, over time and gradually adapting and evolving based on its experiences, there are many types of machine learning that impact the way the program achieves this, to name the most common types:

  • Supervised learning, the developer must monitor and assist the machine in order to train it to work on its own by providing it with labelled training data sets.
  • unsupervised learning, similar to the above but without labelled training data sets.
  • reinforcement learning, the system learns almost entirely on its own

Machine learning is used in an extreme amount of systems and programs that are used on a daily basis in all areas of technology.

  • Virtual assistants on your computer and mobile devices use machine learning to detect when you are talking to them, as well as collect and utilize data based on your day to day interactions with them in order to better understand your needs and give results better tailored to your preferences.
