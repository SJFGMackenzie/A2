Project Idea

Written by Thomas Kauran & Jo Stammers

Overview

An idea for a project which aims to provide all Defense and Emergency Services Personnel with a secure meeting place to chat with likeminded people and others that understand the trauma only they usually experience. The idea is to provide a space with a general area where everybody can communicate, further providing a private end-to-end encrypted space where one-on-one and group private chats transpire. The following sections will discuss in further detail the motivation behind the project, and a detailed description of the project idea; followed by a detailed description on the tools and technologies that will be required to build this project.

Motivation

We understand that those with careers in Defense and Emergency Services alike go through experiences not many outside of those professions would ever experience. These professions though, see trauma repeatedly on a regular basis. AIHW (2021) reported ex-serving defense personnel have suicide rates much higher than the Australian population, with males at a rate of 24% higher and an astonishing rate of 102% higher in females. Moreover, Police health (no date) reported the Beyond Blue research showed a first responder takes their life every six weeks. However, despite these figures, these two professions have the least of mental health service provided to them. Very few services are dedicated to Defense, and most of what are available are government provided. Emergency services do have more on offer specific to them than defense, however still these services are limited for the percentage that suffer from profession related mental health. What they don't have, at all, is a place to be able to talk to others that understand what they do, the traumas, and the things just no-one else can understand, not even a professional psychologist. Our idea is to provide this service specific to Defense and Emergency Service Personnel.

Description

Proposed Idea

A Social Communication Platform that is specific to Defense Force Personnel and Emergency Services Personnel exclusively so they can connect with like -minded people to support mental health for these professions. Users will have access to a public server which will be an all-access area, with proposed 'notice board' type area which will provide links to online mental health assistance services and phone numbers for help and assistance lines relative to our target audience specifically. Additional to the main server, will have access to private one-on-one conversation, or chat groups. Further, the ability to create their own space that is for a particular topic or interest, eg. Fellow ARMY veterans that may want a space to be just ARMY, there maybe some police from an area that know each other might like to have their own area to be able to communicate and share in a space that is just them.

The design of the user experience has not been considered too much at this point of the project planning; However, one idea was to present it in a 'Hotel' style, with a 'Main Lobby' (being the general server), with a 'Noticeboard' (containing the mental health assistance links and phone lines), 'conference room' which would be the (private server areas); and 'Hotel Rooms' would be their (one-on-one or group-chat communication areas). Next, name it “Bravo Mike Hotel Services” (Mike = Mental, Hotel = Health).

Proposed Production

It will be a web-based application that will offer its target audience a way to securely communicate online both privately and publicly with like-minded individuals seeking help. The platform will be built using a range of technologies to build the platform. The team currently believes that to build this project from scratch, it will require a web-based front-end powered by HTML, CSS and JavaScript, back-end services such as a web-API to service the front-end, Matrix API for handling the chat rooms, server lobbies, and all social communications in general.

The front-end and back-end code will then be hosted online through a cloud service provider such as AWS, Azure or Google Cloud. Although the team has not concluded which cloud provider to go with yet, and therefore have not fully scoped out which technology to use from any of those providers, it is somewhat safe to assume that this stage that we would require to procure a storage bucket for our front-end code and other static content such as images. It is also safe to assume that back-end will likely be run on a server instance, however some back-end code may be delegated to serverless technologies also, depending on cost constraints and performance requirements.

Furthermore, the application will require access to data storage services for storing credentials and personal information of users, although undecided by the team at this stage, the two best options that come to mind are SQL and NoSQL Databases.

  1. Users should be able to create their own accounts, edit their personal information on those accounts.
  2. Users should be able to delete their accounts and all records pertaining to their personal information that is saved by the system. Chat records in public servers will stay, however all one-to-one communication isn't saved anywhere except between the two clients who communicated with one another.
  3. Users should be able to add other users as friends for purposes such as remembering details of one another, making it easier to start a one-to-one communication channel etc.
  4. Users should be able to block other users so that they will not be able to be contacted by those users with whom they do not wish to communicate with.
  5. Users should be able to create one-to-one communication with both text and VoIP services.
  6. Users should be able to create their own communities (public servers) so that they can be part of a group who share the same interests, hobbies etc. And can communicate through these public servers using both text and VoIP.
  7. Users should be able to administrate their own communities (servers) and provide administrative rights to users they trust.
  8. Users should be able to delete their communities (servers)

Looking to the future of the application after a minimum viable product has been created, it would be possible to add additional features such as incorporating other services into the application, such as bringing in data from WhatsApp, Discord, Facebook, Slack and more thanks to Matrix's bridging features.

Cloud Infrastructure

We will be using SaaS cloud services to provide the service to the proposed public. SaaS or Software as a Service is a distribution model hosted by cloud service providers. The service providers we might consider are Amazon Web Services (AWS); Google Cloud Services (GCS); or Microsoft (Azure). The SaaS service, hosted by one or more of these providers allows us to deploy software on their infrastructure. We would be responsible for building our software; granting access for intended users; creating and managing accounts; and resource provisioning (AWS, no date). While the service provider manages all Hardware and software including app software and middleware, and even provides security (Oracle, no date).

Cloud services diagram

When choosing a cloud service provider, there are things that should be considered. Firstly, is making sure the service meets the expectation of what you want to achieve; In our circumstance we want a service that allows us to deploy an end user software that provides a social communication web application. Secondly, the reliability of the provider should be taken into consideration; a provider with a high availability will generally have some level of disaster recovery and fault tolerance in response to major disaster; Their uptime and down time is important to consider, taking note of how they measure (Stephenson, no date).

Significantly, cloud services are not free of cost; therefore, pricing would be a factor we must consider when choosing a cloud service provider. We would need to consider the method in which each provider calculates their pricing, for the services they would be providing; Also to consider is, if there will be additional costs for a service not included or are they flexible to tailor a plan. The particular cloud services we will require cannot possibly be known until we get further into planning in upcoming assignment 3. Therefore, further detail into the factors around which service providers we would consider, will be expanded on within assignment 3.
