Group Reflections

Group Reflection

What went well?

As a group we believe we have achieved a great amount of success with our second assignment, as a team we have spent many hours both in meetings and individually working towards reaching a project idea that not only meets the minimum requirements but goes beyond what is expected.

When a team member has had struggles completing a designated task, one or more members have been there to aid us, in this regard one member has stood out, Shay and we appreciate the hard work she has put in to be the clue that holds the group together.

What could be improved?

We believe that it is important for the group learn our tools better so that we may have an easier time communicating with one another, but also in creating more outstanding work within our third assignment. The group will be dedicating some of their time individually to learning Microsoft teams, Trello, and GitHub to further their skills not only in this subject, but for their future studies and careers in IT.

At least one thing that was surprising

Unfortunately, on Tuesday the 12th of July 2022, the team's leader Stephan left the Group's Microsoft Teams without saying a word or messaging a member. The group found this too surprising to believe at first because it was what we all believed to be out of character for Stephan. We emailed Anthony asking for him to contact Stephan to find out what had happened, and if everything was alright as the team believed something must have gone wrong, and that he may not have been ok.

To the groups surprise however Anthony contacted Stephan and it turns out he could no longer continue to study with the group for reasons unknown to us. This has been difficult for the group and caused many issues for us as he left in the final week before submission for assignment two.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups

For most of the group, this is their first-time being part of a group in university studies. Two members, Jo and Thomas have previously been in a few project-based subjects and have already experienced the lows and highs that come with working within a group. So as a team the majority of us have learned that communication can be an issue sometimes, some team members may take offense and leave if a misunderstanding occurs and, in the end it is best to be clear and concise with one another to come together as a group and produce the best work we can together.

Jo Stammers

What went well?

Our team was formed quite quickly, in fact, four members had already teamed up prior to it being necessary, the other 2 added early in week 4 (first week of A2) and this happened during our course-wide lecture with Anthony and he confirmed our group. I realised on the weekend of week 4 we were supposed to have at least 1 recorded meeting per week, so quickly arranged a time available to everyone and our first meeting was successfully held on the Sunday of week 4. From there we held meetings twice a week on set days of Tuesday and Sunday to which everyone usually attended, except a couple of incidental times.

What could be improved?

From a perspective of RMIT, I believe tutors need to be a bit more involved in group processes. And staying up to date with where each of their students are at, possibly involvement in a designated number of team's meetings. From a student perspective; it becomes difficult when such a variety of ages, interests, experience and many other factors are a major part of online study. I found most members basically submitted work that was not answering directly what was asked. Some parts I agree are difficult to really understand what It's asking, but I feel that lack of caring to understand come into it also.

I was disappointed that a couple of things I pointed out in meetings, posts and other conversations the importance of referencing and even gave example and a format for everyone to use. Only 2 members, including myself, provided work initially with in-text referencing and just the website provided as the reference list. 2 members which I picked up early, I asked them to go back and fix up referencing, which they further provided updated, referenced work. So, communication, and uploading of work, as you go is beneficial to all team members.

At least one thing that was surprising

Just one thing? Because we got a double whammy! 5 days before original submission date our team leader acutely left our group and deleted work in teams. This left us with a lot of work remaining, running low on time, and members starting to mis deadlines for submissions. We were granted an extension to Tues 19th Jul, so we had made a plan to get work completed, and then on Monday, the day before submission, we unfortunately held trust in a member we thought was reliable, and happily accepted taking on extra work to help out others; to be faced with quite a large portion of work undelivered and another member uncontactable over the 2 days leading up to submission, which left myself and Thomas to pick up a LOT of extra work that has not been completed in pressing time to submission.

However, a positive surprise was meeting 1 person I work and learn well with/from, Thomas, who has prior education and experience, and is near end of his Bachelor, has been a wealth of information and the only reason we have a website.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups?

Well, I can't say I learned much, I have had similar teamwork experiences in previous study, and so nothing that happened was really surprising to me, I just reverted to dealing with it by taking everything on myself, neglecting myself of sleep and nourishment or relaxation and even taken time off work to complete this assignment. I guess, I didn't learn it but confirmed that studying in teams, especially early on in a course you are trying to learn new things, is not a nice experience.

Gianni
